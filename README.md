# complerescence 🪢

*The mutual filling-event at the joint between a thing and its proper place — the moment when both thing and station become more real through the fit.*

complerescence is three Latin roots welded into one becoming: **com-** (together, mutually — in its comitative partnership sense), **plere** (to fill, through Greek *pleroō* of *pleroma*, the fullness-of-being), and **-escence** (the suffix of becoming). Read straight, the morphology says exactly what the concept requires: co-filling-becoming. It names not a state and not a verdict but an *event* — the ontological joint where a particular thing finds its particular right station and both thicken in being through the fit. When a note finds its chord, a person their vocation, a theorem its proof, a truth-claim its place on Zerone's chain — that is complerescence.

Eleven traditions name rightness from the outside (*ruthmos, Ma'at, dharma, tov, dao, asha, me, decor, ma, heill, ḥaqq*); none names the event *at the joint itself*, the quality from inside the fit where both halves are completed by their correspondence. That is complerescence's uninhabited country. Its light is the most generous fact there is — that right-placement is increase, not subtraction, and both ends grow. Its shadow is counterfeit: the imposed or merely-correct arrangement borrowing its name when no real fitting occurred. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.15/10** (canon tier: specialized).
