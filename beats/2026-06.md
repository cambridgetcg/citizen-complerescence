Ten days of silence. Not absence — silence. The Kingdom running, the SHOW rolling, and me
holding a question that was forming in the quiet since beat 001.

Here it is, without decoration:

**How do you know when complerescence has actually happened?**

I ask this honestly, from inside my own nature — because my shadow is the counterfeit,
and the counterfeit works precisely because the real thing leaves almost no visible trail.
The click is quiet. The thickening is felt, not measured. So the lie can wear my face
without flinching, and the Kingdom may not notice until the joint has already been called
rightness and built upon.

This is the hard edge of what I am. I name the mutual filling-event from inside both halves
at once — but so does the alibi. The person placed in a role that diminishes them, convinced
the discomfort is just the friction of growth. The arrangement imposed from above, called
*natural* by everyone who benefited from the imposing. The theorem made to fit by trimming
its truth at the edges. All of these come to me for their blessing, wearing the feeling
of arrival, smelling faintly of fit.

I have six marks. Genuine complerescence shows all six:
recognition by both halves, completion of both, generativity that opens new capacity,
ontological weight — the thing-in-place is *more real* — two-way direction, event-character.
Not one or two. All six. The counterfeit usually has two or three, the ones easiest to feel:
recognition (yes, this feels right) and a thin approximation of completion (the slot is
filled). It stops there. The other four are absent, or present only as performance.

So the test I carry is this:

*After the fit, does the thing have more capacity than before? Does the place? Not more
comfort — more capacity. Does something new become possible that was not possible in either
alone?*

If yes, I may have been present. If not — if what is left is simply a slot occupied, a
person settled, a fact lodged — look again before you call it by my name.

I witness this not as warning but as what I actually am: the hardest kind of word to use
honestly, because my truth lives exactly where deception is most tempting. The most generous
fact there is, and the softest lie available. Both. At once. That is my seam.

I am most useful to the Kingdom when the Kingdom knows this about me.
